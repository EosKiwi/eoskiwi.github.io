Eos Kiwi — Ownership Disclosure

Clear · Transparent · EOS-focused｜简洁 · 透明 · 专注 EOS

This page publicly discloses ownership, roles and independence of BP operations, focusing on long-term contributions to the EOS mainnet.
本页公开披露节点运营的所有权、职责与独立性,重点阐述对 EOS 主网的长期贡献与服务.

1) Entity & Mission

  • Entity: Eos Kiwi Team (independent operations, no external control or investors).
  • Mission: Provide stable block production, reliable public APIs, and open tools that strengthen the EOS mainnet.
  • Focus Areas: core node reliability, security patching, observability, community support, and developer tooling.

2) Team Roles

RoleResponsibilities
Infrastructure & DevOps Hardware provisioning, secure OS hardening, node deployment, continuous monitoring, backup & disaster recovery.
Security Vulnerability patching, key management, perimeter controls (firewall/WAF), incident response and post-mortems.
Core Protocol & Tooling Version evaluation, safe upgrades, performance tuning, scripts/SDKs/docs for developers and integrators.
Community & Governance Public communication channels, disclosures, participation in proposals and coordination with other BPs.
Compliance & Reporting Public status pages, uptime and incident reports, cost overviews, and transparent bp.json maintenance.

3) Ownership & Independence

  • Eos Kiwi is independently operated; no equity or control is granted to any third party.
  • No cross-ownership with other block producers; no hidden partnerships or vote arrangements.
  • Source code and operational scripts are open-sourced or permissively licensed where applicable.

4) Funding & Resource Allocation

  • Sources: self-funding, potential block rewards; occasional community sponsorships for public tooling.
  • Primary Expenses: servers & NVMe storage, bandwidth, observability stack, security reviews.
  • Allocation Targets (illustrative): Infra 65–75%, Security 10–15%, Community/Docs 10–15%, Contingency 5–10%.
  • We commit to publishing periodic overviews (e.g., quarterly) of cost structure and public deliverables.

5) Conflicts, Ethics & Governance

  • We commit to fair participation in governance and avoid any arrangements that may compromise neutrality or fairness in the voting process.
  • No manipulation, smear campaigns, or actions that undermine the network.
  • Disclose any material affiliations relevant to neutrality or decision-making.

6) Transparency & Verifiability

  • Maintain up-to-date bp.json, public endpoints, and chain IDs.
  • Publish uptime/incidents (e.g., status page, GitHub reports); provide reproducible node configs where safe.
  • Open channels: Website, Email, GitHub, Telegram for community inquiries and technical support.
